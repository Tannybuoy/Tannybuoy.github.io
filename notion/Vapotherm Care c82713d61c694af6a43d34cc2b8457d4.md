# Vapotherm Care

Project Description: How might we bolster psychological safety for those managing COPD at home?
My Role: Data Analysis, Product Management, UI Design
Team Size: 6

## Brief

Vapotherm, a global healthcare leader is looking to expand their market share and differentiate from competitors by offering telehealth solutions. The problem statement involves exploring solutions and services for **at-home COPD management** by patients who currently require non-invasive ventilation. 

---

## Process

The solution was built by going through the new product development process. It consisted of multiple phases - Discovery, Research, Design and Prototyping. 

![Untitled](Untitled%204.png)

- **Discovery** Understanding the current landscape of the market through PESTLE analysis, affinity diagramming, stakeholder mapping, positioning map, concept diagram.
- **Research** Deep dive into the problem space and conductive primary and secondary research to understand the core problems by listening to social chatter, conducting patient and physician interviews, attending webinars, and more.
- **Design** Brainstorming solutions which build upon the research and identifying parameters to evaluate success like creative matrix, round robin brainstorming.
- **Prototyping** Running buildathons to allow for rapid prototyping, and then running usability testing sessions and incorporating client feedback before the final Hi-fi prototype.

## Solution

The final solution was a community-centric mobile application which encouraged communication between clinicians, patients and others with COPD. 

- It provided **access to resources** and encouraged daily routine building through customized to-do lists and buddy groups.
- Concept of **buddy groups**, which would be an intimate group of 6 members that would check-in regularly and encourage each other.
- Regular **notifications** prompting action on the to-do list defined through collaboration between physician and patient and allowing progress tracking.

## Reflections

- **Finding the right participants** is key to gathering good data**.** It is important to understand what kind of questions need to be answered to find the right fit.

- Getting **continuous feedback** is important for team ownership and stakeholder buy-in in the final solution.
