Microsoft Teams Chat

Facilitate private conversations with another participant through the Meeting Chat tab during video calls.

Product Management UI Design User Experience Design

Brief

There are multiple ways to send someone a message on Microsoft Teams, both in private or in a group setting. The project explored how we might quickly contact someone during a Microsoft Teams video call.

This project was done for the course Designing Smart Systems, which focused on the design of systems that learn and adapt as they gather more information over time.

Approach

My approach involved a mixture of primary research and secondary research, followed by designing iterations and testing them with users to get feedback, balanced with a cost savings analysis.

Research

270M Monthly active users of Microsoft Teams worldwide (2022)
91 Fortune 100 companies use Microsoft Teams
230K+ Educational institutions worldwide on the platform

I onboarded regular users of video calling platforms through a survey and ran usability testing sessions to gauge differences across platforms.

Platform Send DM through Meeting Chat? What comes first? Search in Meeting Chat?
Teams No Participant No
Zoom Yes Either Drop down
Google Meet No None No
Gather Yes Either Search bar

Solution

I started with three simple ideas to incorporate the proposed considerations in different ways. The final solution was chosen because it addressed key usability gaps:

  • Search bar that lists participants to ensure recognition before the need for recall.
  • No chat pop-ups over the incoming video stream, hence not affecting visibility of vital components of a video call.
  • Semi-persistent visibility of previous chats done during the meeting with full name visibility and preview of previous chats.
  • The user has the flexibility of keeping the direct chat drop down open or not.

Business Impact

I ideated quantitative metrics that could be measured, benchmarked before feature roll-out and after, while ensuring no other platform changes could affect the numbers.

$9 Cost savings per professional per year for businesses

The proposed design serves as an important starting point to run a deeper and large-scale effort into analyzing participants' chatting habits during meetings.

Reflections

1

Big platforms don't have big problems. Each problem seems small, but collectively if solved can lead to high returns.

2

Form and Function are interconnected. UI ≠ UX is true, but it might be better put as UI ⊂ UX. The interface design is a subset of the entire user experience.

3

Always be cognizant of User's Mental Models. Careful consideration needs to be given since mental models become rigid and users become resistant to change.
