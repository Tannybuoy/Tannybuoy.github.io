Healthcare Telehealth

Vapotherm Care

How might we bolster psychological safety for those managing COPD at home?

Data Analysis Product Management UI Design Team of 6

Brief

Vapotherm, a global healthcare leader, is looking to expand their market share and differentiate from competitors by offering telehealth solutions. The problem statement involves exploring solutions and services for at-home COPD management by patients who currently require non-invasive ventilation.

Process

The solution was built by going through the new product development process, consisting of multiple phases: Discovery, Research, Design, and Prototyping.

  • Discovery: Understanding the current landscape through PESTLE analysis, affinity diagramming, stakeholder mapping, positioning map, and concept diagram.
  • Research: Deep dive into the problem space through primary and secondary research — listening to social chatter, conducting patient and physician interviews, attending webinars, and more.
  • Design: Brainstorming solutions building upon research and identifying parameters to evaluate success using creative matrix and round robin brainstorming.
  • Prototyping: Running buildathons for rapid prototyping, then running usability testing sessions and incorporating client feedback before the final hi-fi prototype.

Insights

Psychological Safety

A critical finding that shaped our thinking was how large a role psychological safety played in the definition of quality of life for those living with COPD. It was insightful learning about the interplay between psychological safety and other indicators of a quality life.

Competitive Analysis

Understanding the space and products currently out there helped us identify potential opportunity areas that Vapotherm can target, since Vapotherm is looking to build a sustainable differentiation.

Brainstorming

We wrote the problem statement on the board and that was the space we ideated in. Keeping our learnings and requirements on a backburner, we did quick brainstorming sessions — sketching out 8 ideas in 8 minutes.

User Feedback

Using Facebook was the way to reach people with COPD and engaging them to understand if the proposed solution could be integrated into their daily lives easily and have value.

"To post our goals I feel is important if for no other reason than to give us a reason to live."

Solution

The final solution was a community-centric mobile application that encouraged communication between clinicians, patients, and others with COPD.

  • Provided access to resources and encouraged daily routine building through customized to-do lists and buddy groups.
  • Concept of buddy groups — an intimate group of 6 members that would check-in regularly and encourage each other.
  • Regular notifications prompting action on the to-do list, defined through collaboration between physician and patient, allowing progress tracking.

Reflections

1

Finding the right participants is key to gathering good data. It is important to understand what kind of questions need to be answered to find the right fit.

2

Prototyping is an underrated phase that done well can give key customer insights that other types of research cannot showcase.

3

Getting continuous feedback is important for team ownership and stakeholder buy-in in the final solution.
